Galaxy Explorer

Scroll through the universe — one galaxy at a time.

Milky Way

Our home spiral galaxy with over 200 billion stars. The Sun resides in one of its spiral arms.

Andromeda

The nearest spiral galaxy, set to collide with the Milky Way in 4 billion years.

Triangulum

The third-largest galaxy in our Local Group (after Andromeda and the Milky Way). A spiral galaxy located about 2.7 million light-years away. It may be gravitationally bound to Andromeda.

Large Magellanic Cloud

An irregular dwarf galaxy and a satellite of the Milky Way. It’s the fourth-largest galaxy in the Local Group and home to the famous Tarantula Nebula, one of the most active star-forming regions.

Small Magellanic Cloud

Another irregular dwarf galaxy near the LMC, also orbiting the Milky Way. Both LMC and SMC are visible in the southern hemisphere and are important laboratories for studying early galaxy evolution.

Messier 87 (M87)

A giant elliptical galaxy in the Virgo Cluster, located about 53 million light-years from Earth. Famous for containing a supermassive black hole, which was the first ever to be photographed in 2019 by the Event Horizon Telescope.

Whirlpool

A classic spiral galaxy interacting with a smaller companion. Located about 31 million light-years away in the constellation Canes Venatici. It's a popular target in astronomy due to its distinct spiral structure.

Sombrero

Named for its hat-like appearance, this spiral galaxy has a bright central bulge and a large halo. Located about 29 million light-years away in the constellation Virgo.

Centaurus A

A peculiar galaxy with both elliptical and spiral features. It is known for intense radio emissions and a supermassive black hole at its center.

IC 1101

The largest known galaxy in the observable universe, located over 1 billion light-years away. It’s a supergiant elliptical galaxy and can be up to 6 million light-years across.
